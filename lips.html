HOW TO DRAW A LIPS

Drawing lips can be a rewarding experience, This tutorial will guide you through the process. Here's a step-by-step tutorial for drawing a simple, realistic Lips.

  • Materials You'll Need

    • Pencil
    • Eracer
    • Paper

      • Step 1: Basic Structure

        1. Draw two crossing lines: Start by drawing a horizontal and vertical line intersecting at the center. The horizontal line represents the alignment of the lips, and the vertical line helps with symmetry.
        2. Outline the general shape: Draw a rough outline of the lips' shape, keeping in mind that the top lip is usually thinner and more curved than the bottom lip.

      • Step 2: Refining the Shape/h3>

        1. Define the cupid's bow: On the upper lip, mark out the curves that form the cupid's bow. They typically resemble an "M" shape.
        2. Outline the lower lip: The lower lip has a more rounded shape. Draw it with a soft curve..
        3. Corners of the mouth: Refine the corners of the mouth by extending the top lip slightly beyond the bottom lip.

      • Step 3: Adding Details

        1. Inner lines: Add an inner line in the center of the lips to represent where they meet. This line can be soft or slightly curved depending on the expression you're aiming for.
        2. Outline the lips: Make the outline of the lips more refined and clean. The upper lip tends to have sharper edges, while the lower lip is softer.

      • Step 4: Shading

        1. Start shading the upper lip: Use soft, short strokes to shade the upper lip, especially towards the center where the lips meet. The upper lip is usually darker because of the natural shadow cast by the shape of the lips.
        2. Shade the bottom lip: The bottom lip catches more light, so the shading here should be softer. Add shading to the lower corners and under the lip for a realistic look.

      • Step 5: Blending

        1. Smooth the shading: Use a blending tool or your finger to gently blend the shading on the lips for a softer, more natural look.
        2. Highlight areas: Leave the middle of the lower lip lighter to represent the natural shine lips often have.

      • Step 6: Final Touches

        1. Deepen shadows: Darken the corners of the mouth and where the lips meet for more depth.
        2. Add highlights: Add small highlights using an eraser or a white pencil to the middle of the lower lip and the upper lip's bow to make them look shiny and three-dimensional.
