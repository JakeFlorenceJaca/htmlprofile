HOW TO DRAW A DOG

Back to gallery

  • Materials You'll Need

    • Pencil/Pen
    • Eracer
    • Paper

      • Step 1: Basic Shape

        1. Start by drawing a basic oval shape. This will form the overall shape of the dog's head.
        2. Draw a vertical line down the center of the oval. This will help you keep the dog's head symmetrical.
        3. Draw a horizontal line across the middle of the oval. This will help you place the eyes and nose.

      • Step 2: Eyes and Nose

        1. Draw two almond-shaped eyes on either side of the vertical line, slightly below the horizontal line.
        2. Draw a triangular shape below the eyes for the nose.
        3. Draw two curved lines extending from the outer edges of the eyes downwards to form the cheekbones.

      • Step 3: Ears and Muzzle

        1. Draw two triangular shapes above the eyes for the ears.
        2. Draw a curved line below the nose to form the muzzle.

      • Step 4: Fur and Details

        1. Draw the fur around the dog's head and ears. You can use short, curved lines to create a realistic look.
        2. Add details to the eyes, such as the pupils and eyelids.
        3. Add details to the nose, such as the nostrils.

      • Step 5: Shading

        1. Use shading to create depth and dimension in the dog's fur and face.
        2. You can use cross-hatching or other shading techniques to create texture and detail.

      • Tips:

        1. Use light pencil strokes at first, so you can easily erase any mistakes.
        2. Practice drawing different breeds of dogs to learn about their unique features.
        3. Observe real dogs or photos of dogs for reference.
        4. Don't be afraid to experiment with different shading techniques.

      • Remember, practice makes perfect! The more you draw, the better you will become.
