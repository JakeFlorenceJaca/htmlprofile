HOW TO DRAW A ROOSTER

Back to gallery

  • Materials You'll Need

    • Pencil/Pen
    • Eracer
    • Paper
    • Optional: Colored pencils or markers

      • Step 1: Basic Shapes

        1. Start by drawing a small circle for the head.
        2. Below it, draw a larger oval for the body.
        3. Add two curved lines extending from the head to form the neck.

      • Step 2: Add Details to the Head

        1. Draw a comb on top of the head, shaped like a flame or a crown.
        2. Add a beak with a small circle for the nostril.
        3. Draw two eyes, which can be simple circles or ovals with smaller circles inside for pupils.

      • Step 3: Draw the Tail Feathers

        1. From the lower part of the body, draw a series of curved lines that gradually increase in length to form the tail feathers.
        2. Add details to the feathers by drawing smaller lines within the larger ones.

      • Step 4: Add the Wings

        1. Draw two curved lines extending from the sides of the body to form the wings.
        2. Add details to the wings by drawing smaller lines within the larger ones.

      • Step 5: Draw the Legs and Feet

        1. Draw two legs extending from the lower part of the body.
        2. Each leg should have two segments: the thigh and the shank.
        3. At the end of each shank, draw four toes with claws.

      • Step 6: Add Details and Shading (Optional)

        1. If you want to add more details, you can draw feathers on the neck, breast, and back.
        2. You can also add shading to create a 3D effect. Use darker lines to shade the areas that would be darker in real life, such as the underside of the body and the shadows between feathers.

      • Additional Tips:

        1. Practice drawing different shapes and lines to improve your skills.
        2. Don't worry about making mistakes. Mistakes can be erased or corrected.
        3. Have fun and experiment with different styles and techniques.
        4. Remember, this is just one way to draw a rooster. You can also find other tutorials and resources online to learn different techniques.
