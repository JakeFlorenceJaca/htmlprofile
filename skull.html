HOW TO DRAW A SKULL

Back to gallery

  • Materials You'll Need

    • Pencil/Pen
    • Eracer
    • Paper

      • Step 1: Basic Shape

        1. Start by drawing a basic oval shape. This will form the overall shape of the skull.
        2. Draw a vertical line down the center of the oval. This will help you keep the skull symmetrical.
        3. Draw a horizontal line across the middle of the oval. This will help you place the eye sockets and nasal cavity.

      • Step 2: Eye Sockets and Nasal Cavity

        1. Draw two almond-shaped eye sockets on either side of the vertical line, slightly below the horizontal line.
        2. Draw a triangular shape below the eye sockets for the nasal cavity.

      • Step 3: Cheekbones and Jawline

        1. Draw two curved lines extending from the outer edges of the eye sockets downwards to form the cheekbones.
        2. Connect the bottom of the cheekbones with a curved line to form the jawline.

      • Step 4: Teeth and Mouth

        1. Draw a curved line inside the lower jaw to represent the gumline.
        2. Draw a row of small, pointed teeth along the gumline.
        3. Draw a curved line above the teeth to represent the upper lip.

      • Step 5: Skull Details

        1. Draw the details of the skull, such as the brow ridges, the nasal bone, and the zygomatic arches (cheekbones).
        2. Add shading to define the form and create depth.

      • Step 6: Finishing Touches

        1. Erase any unnecessary lines.
        2. Add shading to highlight the form and create a sense of volume.
        3. You can add cross-hatching or other shading techniques to create texture and detail.

      • Tips:

        1. Use light pencil strokes at first, so you can easily erase any mistakes.
        2. Practice drawing different angles and perspectives of the skull.
        3. Observe real skulls or images of skulls for reference.
        4. Don't be afraid to experiment with different shading techniques.

        Remember, practice makes perfect! The more you draw, the better you will become.
